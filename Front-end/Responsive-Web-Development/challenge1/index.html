Longping Yuan (袁隆平)

Father of Hybird Rice: The man who solve the hunger problems of billions of people
Yuan is reading the newspaper in 1962.

    Here's a time line of Mr. Yuan's life:

  • 1930 - Born in Beijing, China
  • 1953 - Graduated from Southwest Agricultural College and began his teaching career at an agriculture school in Anjiang, Hunan Province, China
  • 1960s - Came up with an idea for hybridizing rice when a series of natural disasters and harmful political policies had plunged China into an unprecedented famine that caused the deaths of millions of Chinese citizens
  • 1964 ~ 1966 - Created his theory and proved it.
  • 1973 - Established a complete process of creating and reproducing high-yield hybrid rice species.

    Contributions:

  • 1979 - Hybrid rice was introduced into the United States, making it the first case of intellectual property rights transfer in the history of the People's republic of China.
  • 1991 - The United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization statistics show that 20% of the world's rice output came from 10% of the world's rice fields that grow hybrid rice.

    Honors and awards

  • Four asteroids and a college in China have been named after him.
  • Won the State Preeminent Science and Technology Award of China in 2000
  • Won the Wolf Prize in Agriculture and the World Food Prize in 2004
  • Currently the Director-General of the China National Hybrid Rice R&D Center
  • Professor at Hunan Agricultural University, Changsha
  • Member of the Chinese Academy of Engineering
  • Foreign associate of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences(2006) and 2006 CPPCC
  • Chief counsultant of the FAO in 1991
